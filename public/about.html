About Us: NU-CATS Institute

It is an exciting and opportune time for biomedical research at Northwestern. With the NIH Roadmap as a catalyst, we are formally launching the Northwestern University Clinical and Translational Sciences (NU-CATS) Institute. Philip Greenland, MD, executive associate dean for clinical and translational research and Harry W Dingman Professor of Preventive Medicine, will direct the Institute, and William Lowe, MD, professor of medicine, will serve as deputy director.

The NU-CATS Institute will become the home base for clinical and translational science at Northwestern University and its clinical affiliates. It will provide the infrastructure, services, and resources necessary to maximize and leverage our breadth and depth of interdisciplinary "bench to bedside" research.

In its infancy, the NU-CATS Institute is already seizing opportunities to advance and support our translational research priorities. Drs. Greenland and Lowe spearheaded the preparation and submission of a 5-year, $58 million Clinical and Translational Science Award (CTSA) proposal to the National Institutes of Health (NIH) in January. Translational research is very much a priority at the NIH, and the CTSA program is intended to drive the transformation of academic medical centers. Our proposal was a truly collaborative effort involving faculty from five Northwestern schools, as well as our clinical affiliates Northwestern Memorial Hospital, Children’s Memorial Hospital, Evanston Northwestern Healthcare, and the Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago. In step with the NIH program guidelines, we are creating the following NU-CATS Institute programs to address the unique needs of our clinical and translational research enterprise.

In January, the Institute co-sponsored a successful workshop on biomedical start-ups at the Robert H. Lurie Medical Research Center. In addition, it is planning to send one young Northwestern University investigator to the Clinical Research Forum Annual Meeting in Washington, DC, this April. The Andrew Senyei, MD, Translational Research Awards, which were created through the generosity of medical alumnus and University Trustee Andrew Senyei, MD, also will be coordinated through the NU-CATS Institute.
